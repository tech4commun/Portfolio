What i am all about.

My journey wasn't about building a suit of armor; it was about building myself. I started B.Tech like a lot of people, a bit of an egomaniac with big ideas and a bigger ego. I saw coding problems as challenges to dominate, not puzzles to solve collaboratively. I thought my intelligence was enough to carry me, but a few botched projects and late-night debugging sessions taught me otherwise.

The real innovation came when I stopped thinking only of myself. When I started helping my friends with their projects and seeing the value in their unique perspectives, I realized true ingenuity isn't a solo act. It's about collaboration, shared passion, and the collective power of a team.

My time in B.Tech is an electrifying journey of a student who is evolving from a self-serving individual into a selfless team player, showcasing the power of shared knowledge and the capacity for growth.
